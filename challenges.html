What is a breadboard?

A breadboard is a board with a bunch of holes (terminals) connected internally in a certain way which makes connecting components or wires together very simple. The breadboard used in Short Circuit VR has two horizontal strips of terminals at the top and at the bottom as well. Each terminal in a horizontal strip is internally connected with the other terminals in that strip. Besides the horizontal strips you’ve got two blocks of 30 vertical strips, each made of 5 terminals. Again each terminal in a strip is connected with all the other terminals in that strip.

How do you use a breadboard?

Usually the battery or some other power source is connected to the horizontal strips, and then some wires will connect the horizontal strips to some vertical strips where components can be placed easier.

image: breadboard with battery connected to horizontal strip and random component on horizontal strips with wires connected.

What is a closed circuit?

For electricity to flow you’ll need a closed circuit as electrons (conventionally!) flow from the positive terminal of the power source to the negative terminal of the power source. But just connecting two power source terminals together will create a too high current and may end up breaking or even burning something. This is called a short circuit and must be avoided at all times.

diagram: indicate flow of electrons on ch1 diagram.

How do you avoid a short circuit?

To restrict the current from flowing this much we can use a resistor. Resistors come in many different values. The higher the value the more it will restrict the current.

Challenge 2: LED

Challenge two asks the player to connect a LED correctly.

image: ch2 tablemat

What is a LED (diode)?

LED is short for Light Emitting Diode. As the name suggests a led is a diode. In general a diode is an electronic component that allows current to flow in one direction but not in the other. So the polarity of this component is important to keep in mind or else no current can pass through the LED and therefore it cannot light up. The terminals of a diode are called Anode and Cathode.

diagram: cathode and anode led

How do you use a LED?

The cathode has to be connected to the negative side of a circuit and the anode to the positive side to have it forward biased.
Besides polarity being an important issue it is equally important to add a resistor to restrict the current. The resistor we need depends on what voltage the power source has that we will be using. A diode needs a specific voltage to be able to work (voltage drop), but too much voltage will break it. A regular LED, as in the application has a voltage drop of 1.8V(check in app to be sure) and a max current of 20mA.

How to calculate the value of the resistor?

To calculate the resistor we will be needing if we use a 9V battery for example, we can calculate it as following using Ohm’s law: (9V-1.8V) = 7.2V -> (7.2V/0.02A) = 360Ω. This means our resistor has to be at least 360 Ohm if we want max brightness. It is always safer to take a resistor with a little higher value. 390 Ohm is the first resistor closest to the value so we could use that. (double check calculations based of values in game)
